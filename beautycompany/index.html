Get going in the great outdoors!

When your child hits grade school, you're left with a narrow window of time in late afternoon when you or her after-school child care provider can encourage your child to exercise.

Build in some time each day to exercise together — just as you try to have a family meal and keep a bedtime routine.
Even venturing out after dinner for a walk to search for stars or chase fireflies counts.
What's most important is that you come up with a game plan that works for your family.

Make your travel plans with physical activity in mind. Look for family-friendly vacation spots that make exercise easy.

Find a cabin by a lake, for instance, that includes a canoe to paddle in, a rope to swing from, and some biking trails to explore.

Plan a summer beach getaway to the shore so you can swim, sail, or boogie board together. Or take a winter break in the snow and go sledding, snow boarding, or cross-country skiing.

Holiday camps that teach new skills — such as horseback riding, cross-country skiing, or roller blading — are also a fun way to get your grade-schooler going.
